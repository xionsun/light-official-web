We are witnessing the emergence of synthetic reality—a unification of physical and digital worlds.

Historically, we delineated two worlds: the physical and the digital. These have already grown quite blurry, and eventually, will disintegrate.

To call this future ‘virtual’ or ‘augmented,’ however, fails to articulate the degree of convergence at play. Rather than constructing another false dichotomy, we prefer a characterization that speaks to the unification of the two formerly distinct worlds: synthetic reality.

Today’s visionaries in gaming are paving the way to these new experiences.

Video game companies have been building the simulation software and concurrency infrastructure that will allow billions to co-experience synthetic reality. For the first time, technological advances and the prevalence of digital natives provide a platform that can bring these bold ambitions to life.

  • Processing and networking advances are enabling more sophisticated, immersive, and social environments.
  • Innovative devices serve as portals to new worlds and sensations.
  • A consumer generation of “digital natives” or “reality agnostics” spends an increasing time in virtual spaces.

Like the Internet, the benefits of synthetic reality extend far beyond entertainment.

The upcoming migration into synthetic reality is not an exercise in mindless escapism. Rather, it presents new opportunities for human fulfillment, ability, and connection. As with synthetic fibers, synthetic reality unites the natural and the artificial to enhance form and function—allowing us to not only immerse ourselves in new forms of entertainment, but transcend the frontiers of human experience, collaboration, and performance.
